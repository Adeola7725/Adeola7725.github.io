Rules of Mancala:

The Board:

  • the board for the Mancala game consists of two rows of 6 pits, one row per player, and two Mancalas, or scoring pools, one for each player
  • the two rows of pits are represented by the buttons, with the numbers showing how many marbles are in each pit
  • the bottom row is the first player's pits, and the top row is the second player's pits
  • the two scoring pools are on the sides, the right side is the first player's pool, and the left side is the second player's pool
  • all the pits start with four marbles, and the scoring pools start with zero points

The Game:

  • at the start of each turn, the player will pick one of their pits to seed
  • the marbles in the pit will be seeded in a counterclockwise direction across the board, with one marble being seeded into each following pit
  • any marbles that are seeded into the player's scoring pool will stay there for the rest of the game
  • the game ends when there are no marbles left on a player's side, and at that point the other player will get all the remaining marbles inside their pits
  • the player with the most marbles in their scoring pool at the end wins the game

Special Rules:

  • If a player's last marble lands in their scoring pool, they get another turn
  • If a player's last marble lands in a previously empty pit on their side, they get the marble from that pit and any marbles in the pit directly opposite
